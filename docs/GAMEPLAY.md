# SimRodman — Gameplay Guide

> Welcome to the Mayor's Office, choomba. Below is everything you need to
> turn an empty plot of irradiated dirt into a sprawling neon megacity.

---

## 0. The Starter City

Every new game spawns with a basic city already in place — a cross of
roads, a Wind Farm + Water Pump, a Holopark, and one strip each of R/C/I
zones. The pieces are gifted (no cost) so your starting funds stay intact.
The camera centers on the cross.

Press **▶** in the topbar to start time and watch the zones boot up. Then
add roads, more power plants, services, and zones from there.

If you want to start completely empty, uncheck **Starter city** in
*Menu → New City…*

## 1. The Map

The world is a **64×64 isometric tile grid**. Tiles are either **water** or
**ground**, with elevation 0–7 on ground tiles. Terrain is procedurally
generated from a noise field, with a winding river carved through it. Each
new city uses a seed (visible in *New City…*) so you can replay the same
layout, or share a seed with a friend.

You cannot build on water (yet — drone-laid platforms coming in v2.078).

## 2. Resources

| Resource     | Symbol | Notes |
|--------------|--------|-------|
| Credits      | ₡      | Money. Earned via taxes, spent on building and maintenance. |
| Population   | POP    | Total residents in residential zones. |
| Jobs         | —      | Total jobs in commercial + industrial. |
| Power        | kW     | Generated by plants, consumed by zones and buildings. |
| Water        | u      | Generated by pumps, consumed similarly. |
| Approval     | APR    | 0–100. Affected by services, tax, demand. |

Approval drops on outages, debt, and high tax. It rises slowly when zones
grow. Below 30%, expect more disasters and slower growth.

## 3. Tools

Pick a tool from the left toolbar, then click or drag the map.

### Inspect
Click a tile to open the info panel (population, density, services,
pollution, crime, land value).

### Demolish (`B`)
Removes whatever is on the tile. Buildings are removed entirely (whole
footprint).

### Roads
- **Road** (`R`) — ₡10. Standard. Carries power, water, and traffic.
- **Neon Highway** (`H`) — ₡30. Same connectivity, brighter glow, double
  maintenance.

### Utilities
- **Power Line** (`P`) — ₡5. Carries power but no traffic. Use to cross
  empty ground between sources and zones.
- **Water Pipe** (`W`) — ₡5. Same, for water.

### Zones
Drag-paint a zone tile (₡10 each). All zones must be **adjacent to a road**
to ever develop. They start at level 0 and grow to level 3 over time as
conditions improve.
- **Residential** (`1`) — provides population.
- **Commercial** (`2`) — provides jobs and weak residential support.
- **Industrial** (`3`) — provides jobs but pollutes.

### Service Buildings
| Tool         | Cost  | Footprint | Provides |
|--------------|-------|-----------|----------|
| Police HQ    | ₡500  | 2×2       | Crime reduction, range 8 |
| Fire Dept.   | ₡500  | 2×2       | Fire risk reduction, range 8 |
| Cyberclinic  | ₡800  | 2×2       | Health coverage, range 10 |
| Datanet School| ₡600 | 2×2       | Education boost, range 9  |
| Holopark     | ₡100  | 1×1       | Land value boost, reduces pollution, range 4 |

All service buildings except Parks need to touch a road.

### Power Plants
| Plant          | Cost    | kW | Pollution | Notes |
|----------------|---------|----|-----------|-------|
| Wind Farm      | ₡2,000  | 50 | 0         | Cheap, low yield. |
| Solar Array    | ₡3,500  | 90 | 0         | Mid-game. |
| Coal Plant     | ₡4,000  | 200| 30        | Cheap kW per ₡, ugly air. |
| Fusion Reactor | ₡18,000 | 700| 2         | Endgame. |

### Water
- **Water Pump** (₡700, 2×2, +200 u). Place near roads or pipes.

### Megastructures
| Building       | Cost    | Effect | Requirements |
|----------------|---------|--------|---------------|
| Megacorp Tower | ₡22,000 | +2,500 jobs | Population ≥ 3,000 |
| Neon Arcology  | ₡35,000 | +8,000 residents, +1,500 jobs | Population ≥ 5,000 |
| Rodman Plaza   | ₡8,000  | City-wide land value & morale boost | Unique (one per city) |

## 4. The Monthly Tick

Time advances in months. Speed buttons in the topbar:

- **II** — Pause
- **▶** — Slow (~0.4 months/sec)
- **▶▶** — Fast (~1.6 months/sec)
- **▶▶▶** — Ultra (~4 months/sec)

Each month, the simulation:

1. Recomputes power and water networks if topology changed.
2. Recomputes service coverage, pollution, crime, and land value fields.
3. Updates zone density: each zone tile computes a fitness score from
   utilities, road access, services, pollution, crime, taxes, and global RCI
   demand, and probabilistically steps level up or down.
4. Computes population and job totals; updates RCI demand.
5. **Payday**: tax income vs. building & road maintenance.
6. Updates approval rating.
7. Rolls a small chance for a disaster (only after pop ≥ 200).
8. Maybe pushes a news headline to the ticker.

## 5. Demand & RCI

The three little bars in the bottom-right are the city's mood:

- **R bar** rises when there are jobs but no homes.
- **C bar** rises when there is residential population but few stores.
- **I bar** rises when residential exists but few factories — and falls when
  it's polluting too much or taxed too high.

Plant zones in proportion. A common starter ratio: **3 R : 2 C : 2 I**.

## 6. Budget & Taxes

Open *Menu → Budget & Taxes*. Slider sets the rate (0–20%). Higher tax =
more income but lower demand.

**Maintenance** is a per-month flat cost: roads, power plants, service
buildings, etc. If your monthly net income is negative for too long, you'll
slip into debt and trigger an alert.

## 7. Ordinances

Optional city-wide policies (toggle in *Menu → Ordinances*):

- **Neon Curfew** — reduces crime, lowers commerce demand.
- **Megacorp Tax Holiday** — boosts industrial demand at ₡200/mo.
- **Air Filtration Mandate** — cuts pollution 25% at ₡150/mo.
- **Cyberware Subsidy** — boosts residential demand at ₡300/mo.

(Ordinances are flags currently — their effects are applied directly in the
demand and field calculations.)

## 8. Disasters

Once population ≥ 200, every month rolls a small chance of disaster:

- **Cyber Attack** — drains ₡800–2300, glitches the screen.
- **Acid Rain** — degrades random buildings.
- **Neon Riot** — sets fire to high-crime blocks.
- **Structure Fire** — random building catches fire.
- **Power Surge** — visual glitch.
- **Drone Swarm** — strafes industrial zones.
- **Quake** — damages random tiles, can break roads.

Fire damage continues over multiple ticks. Higher fire-coverage from Fire
Departments slows the spread.

## 9. Save & Load

- **Autosave** every 30 seconds to `localStorage`.
- *Menu → Save* / *Load* — manual.
- *Menu → Export Save (JSON)* — copy or download a portable backup.
- *Menu → Import Save (JSON)* — paste a backup to restore.
- *Menu → New City…* — start fresh with a custom name, seed, and difficulty.

- [ ] 5,000 population — build an Arcology
